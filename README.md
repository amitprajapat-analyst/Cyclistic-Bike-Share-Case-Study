# Cyclistic-Bike-Share-Case-Study
This case study analyzes historical bike trip data from Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company in Chicago with over 5,800 bicycles and 600 docking stations. As a junior data analyst on the marketing team, the goal was to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently in order to design a data-driven.
Key Insights
1. Total Rides — Members ride more
Annual members take significantly more total rides than casual riders, confirming they are the more profitable segment worth targeting.
2. Day of Week — Different usage patterns
Casual riders peak on weekends (leisure riding) while members ride consistently on weekdays (commuting behavior) — this is the most important finding.
3. Avg Ride Length — Casuals ride longer
Casual riders take longer rides on average than members, suggesting they use bikes for leisure/exploration rather than quick commutes.
4. Bike Types — Preference differences
Both groups use classic and electric bikes, but casual riders show higher preference for electric bikes.

Recommendations

Weekend membership campaigns — Target casual riders on weekends when they're most active with "convert to member and save" messaging
Highlight commute value — Show casuals that a membership pays off if they ride even 3–4 times a week
Electric bike promotions — Since casuals prefer electric bikes, offer membership perks tied to electric bike access
